Introduction:

There're usually four kinds of instruments in a standard symphony orchestra: woodwind, brass, percussion, and string.

Woodwind instruments are most often used to convey emotional subjects, or offer a continuous chord background, due to their rather soft timbre.
Brass instruments, on the other hand, are used to demonstrate military or epic statements, as well as enhancing the stability of tutti of the whole orchestra.
The usage of percussion instrument differs greatly from one to another, but the most common effect is changing the sound quality of the orchestra, i.e. making it massive/passionate/mysterious.
String instruments have the most plasticity among all instruments in the orchestra, making them almost possible to present every kind of emotion or trick you want them to.
